EASEE Uses an Educational Approach

__________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________ 

Our educational efforts explicitly promote sustainable values and awareness of energy efficiency to both help students and their families personally save on their utility bills and help them connect their actions to the environmental benefits of low carbon choices. Our curriculum is simple, active, and age appropriate. Our basic principle is that students become energy experts in a sequence that begins with their own education and then is reinforced when they teach the material to others. The educational experience and information exchange we are developing will lead students to take active steps to reduce energy consumption in their own lives.

High school and college students will be the initial emissaries and mentors laying the foundation of lifelong environmentalism for grammar and middle school students. The younger students will in turn assume the mentoring roles over time and thus sustain the program. 
Training for the high school students acquaints them with basic science, engineering, environmental and economic concepts and processes related to energy, as well as career opportunities in these fields. Once trained, older students will share their knowledge with grammar and middle school students and help them develop practical steps to reduce home energy use. Together, the students will generate enthusiasm and a lifelong commitment to the greening of their earth. 

Note: The EASEE program is designed to satisfy high school service learning and community service requirements. 

Our initial program offering is CFL distribution

Specifically the CFL program presents age appropriate information on energy basics and the environmental impacts of alternative energy production and utilization. Each presentation will last approximately 45 minutes and is geared to the specific grade level that the older students will be teaching (grades one through eight). The student led presentations include:

  1. A general interactive discussion with students about global warming and the state of the environment
  2. Demonstration of a Watt meter to understand the different amounts of energy used by a series of household appliances (e.g. clock, hair dryer); and comparing energy consumption of a compact fluorescent bulb with that of an incandescent bulb. 
  3. Education of the benefits of CFLs and how best to use them at home
  4. General discussion about other ways to reduce energy use at home
  5. Distribution of instructive pamphlet and four CFLs to take home and return with signed validation of their installation.
