EASEE and the CFL – Food Pantry Program

__________________________________________________________________________________________ In keeping with EASEE’s educational approach, the CFL-Food Pantry Program will be an integral part of EASEE’s 2011 programming. With approximately 400,000 families in the Chicago area depending on food pantries, the potential utility savings provided by the CFLs will assist in increasing their available income for other necessities.  The program consists of high school students giving educational presentations to clients of food pantries in the Chicago area about energy, the environment and the benefits of using Compact Fluorescent Lamps (CFLs) in their homes. At the end of the presentation, four CFLs are given to each family, along with information about CFL use and disposal. The CFLs given to each family will provide $200 in electricity bill savings over the coming years. 
Through donations from environmentally aware corporations throughout the Chicago area, EASEE will be able to expand the CFL-Food Pantry Program. Our goal for 2011 is to reach 100,000 families in need with the CFL-Food Pantry Program with a total combined savings of $20 million in electricity costs.
