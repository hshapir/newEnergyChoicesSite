EASEE Uses an Energy Efficiency Approach

______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________
The cheapest, greenest kWh?

 

It's one you don’t use. We don't need to build costly new electricity generation plants, so let's keep them to a minimum.

EASEE actions concentrate on conservation and reduction of total energy usage. Our focus on cost-effective efficiency is a philosophy shared by energy experts as these measures offer immediate economic and environmental benefits. The net benefits are direct reduction of carbon emitted locally by conventional power plants and savings for our student-families. The Department of Energy and numerous management-consulting firms recognize that energy efficiency is the quickest and most cost-effective means to reduce both energy consumption and the emission of greenhouse gases. 

EASEE programs are designed to help families connect every energy decision they make to reducing carbon emissions and lowering utility costs.

 

The impact is local as well as global--students become EASEE energy experts. The students’ active engagement in conscientious, responsible use of energy will foster and fuel a lifelong awareness and commitment to efficiency and environmental action. EASEE is also an economically beneficial program. Our program costs per MWh are lower than Illinois-sourced renewable energy certificates (RECs). However, there are significant differences between investing in EASEE and purchasing Illinois-sourced RECs. Along with educating our youth, EASEE will also bring direct economic benefit to their families. Our initial program will save student-families $150 in avoided electricity expense. 
  

 

Why CFLs?

_____________________________________________________________________________________________
Our initial educational program focuses on using CFLs in student-family homes. The residential market contains 90% of CFL-appropriate sockets, but has only 11% CFL saturation. In Chicago, 40% of families don’t use CFLs.  
Despite increased interest in and demand for energy savings, CFL purchases dropped in 2008, suggesting that additional support may be needed to help sustain awareness and provide direction in how best to use CFLs in homes effectively. 
EASEE’s projects are Illinois based – the investment, economic and environmental benefits are local. 

Of numerous initiatives available to reduce our carbon footprint in Illinois, EASEE's CFL project has the highest carbon-reduction efficacy.
